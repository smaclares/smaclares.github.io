Rocket Girls

The Women "Computers" of NASA's Early History

The pioneers who helped the U.S win the space race.

Before computers, humans, by hand, calculated the astronomical numbers needed to propel rockets into space. Many of these 'human calculators' were women.

They worked during the 1950s, when it was considered that a woman's space was within a domestic domain. These women worked outside the house, created innovative technologies and blazed trails for other women scientists.

Unfortunately, most of these women have faded into obscurity. Even NASA officials cannot name all of the women in its archived photos.
